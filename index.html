Academic Background
In 2006, I received a Bachelor in Cell and Molecolar Biology from the University of Rome Tor Vergata. My main focus was on biochemistry, (Thesis: Characterization of the transmembrane segments of the mitochondrial oxoglutarate carrier). In 2012 I received a Master in Bioinformatics (Thesis: Automatic prediction of the development of the zebrafish embryo, project at the Bio-Imaging Group of Leiden Institute of Advanced Computer Science. My main interest has been in computational biology, databases and Image processing. In 2017, I continued with a PhD in Computer Science and Bioinformatics, applied to the field of biodiversity and evolution. The PhD is a joing collaboration of the Computational Bio-Imaging group, Leiden Institute of Advanced Computer Science (LIACS). and the Understanding Evolution group at the Naturalis Biodiversity Center.
